# Options Strategy Basics

Options add a new dimension to trading: you can profit from direction, volatility, or time decay. This lesson introduces key concepts for quant options strategies.

<ConceptCard title="What Are Options?" type="definition">
An **option** gives you the right (not obligation) to buy or sell an asset at a specific price by a specific date.

**Call**: Right to BUY at strike price
**Put**: Right to SELL at strike price
</ConceptCard>

## The Greeks

<FormulaDisplay

  variables={[

<ConceptCard title="Greek Intuition" type="example">
**Delta = 0.5**: Option moves $0.50 for every $1 move in stock
**Theta = -0.05**: Option loses $0.05 per day from time decay
**Vega = 0.10**: Option gains $0.10 for 1% increase in implied vol
</ConceptCard>

## Volatility Trading

<ConceptCard title="Implied vs Realized" type="tip">
**Implied volatility (IV)**: Market's forecast of future vol (from option prices)
**Realized volatility (RV)**: Actual historical vol

**If IV > RV**: Options are "expensive" → sell options
**If IV < RV**: Options are "cheap" → buy options

This is the basis of volatility arbitrage strategies.
</ConceptCard>

## Common Strategies

<ConceptCard title="Strategy Building Blocks" type="example">
**Directional:**
- Long call/put: Bet on direction with limited downside

**Volatility:**
- Straddle: Long call + long put (profit from big moves)
- Iron condor: Profit from low volatility (range-bound)

**Income:**
- Covered call: Own stock + sell call (earn premium)
- Cash-secured put: Sell put (get paid to buy at lower price)
</ConceptCard>

<KeyTakeaway>
Options enable strategies impossible with stocks alone: profit from **volatility**, **time decay**, or **range-bound** markets. Master the **Greeks** to understand risk, and compare **implied vs realized vol** to find edges. Options add complexity but also opportunity.
</KeyTakeaway>
