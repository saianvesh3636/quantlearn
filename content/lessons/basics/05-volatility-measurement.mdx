# Volatility Measurement

Volatility measures the degree of variation in asset prices. It's the most important risk metric and a key input for position sizing, options pricing, and risk management.

<ConceptCard title="What is Volatility?" type="definition">
**Volatility** is the standard deviation of returns. High volatility means large price swings (risky). Low volatility means stable prices (safer). Unlike return (uncertain), volatility is somewhat predictable.
</ConceptCard>

## Historical Volatility

<FormulaDisplay
  formula="\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n-1}\sum_{i=1}^{n} (r_i - \bar{r})^2}"
  explanation="Calculate volatility from historical returns."
  variables={[
    { symbol: "\\sigma", meaning: "Standard deviation (volatility)" },
    { symbol: "r_i", meaning: "Return in period i" },
    { symbol: "\\bar{r}", meaning: "Mean return" }

/>

<FormulaDisplay
  formula="\sigma_{annual} = \sigma_{daily} \times \sqrt{252}"

## EWMA Volatility

<FormulaDisplay
  formula="\sigma_t^2 = \lambda \sigma_{t-1}^2 + (1 - \lambda) r_{t-1}^2"
  explanation="Exponentially weighted volatility gives more weight to recent observations."
  variables={[
    { symbol: "\\lambda", meaning: "Decay factor (typically 0.94 for daily)" },
    { symbol: "r_{t-1}", meaning: "Most recent return" }
  ]}
/>

<ConceptCard title="Why EWMA?" type="tip">
**EWMA** reacts faster to volatility changes than simple historical volatility. When markets suddenly get volatile, EWMA adjusts quickly. RiskMetrics uses λ = 0.94 for daily data.
</ConceptCard>

## Volatility Clustering

<ConceptCard title="Volatility is Persistent" type="warning">
**Key insight**: High volatility tends to follow high volatility. Low volatility tends to follow low volatility. This **clustering** means tomorrow's volatility is somewhat predictable from today's.
</ConceptCard>

## GARCH Models

<FormulaDisplay
  formula="\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha r_{t-1}^2 + \beta \sigma_{t-1}^2"
  explanation="GARCH captures volatility clustering and mean reversion."
  variables={[
    { symbol: "\\omega", meaning: "Long-run variance baseline" },
    { symbol: "\\alpha", meaning: "Reaction to recent shock" },
    { symbol: "\\beta", meaning: "Persistence of volatility" }
  ]}
/>

<ConceptCard title="GARCH Intuition" type="example">
- **α** controls how much a big move today affects tomorrow's volatility
- **β** controls how long elevated volatility persists
- **α + β < 1** ensures volatility eventually returns to normal
</ConceptCard>

## Implied Volatility

<ConceptCard title="Market's Forecast" type="definition">
**Implied volatility (IV)** is backed out from option prices. It represents what the market expects future volatility to be. The VIX index is the implied volatility of S&P 500 options.
</ConceptCard>

<KeyTakeaway>
Volatility measures risk through return variability. Use **historical volatility** for simple calculations, **EWMA** for faster reaction, and **GARCH** for sophisticated forecasting. **Implied volatility** from options reflects market expectations. Remember: volatility clusters - high vol follows high vol.
</KeyTakeaway>
